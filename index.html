CTR: RACE FOR EARTH
A Twine Story · Adventure Mode
Progress
🛸 Alien Ultimatum
Hub World · Opening

A shadow falls across every racetrack on Earth. The alien racer Nitros Oxide descends from orbit in his saucer, broadcasting a challenge on every frequency:

"Earthlings! I am the fastest being in the universe. Race me — and WIN — or I turn your miserable planet into a parking lot!"

The world's greatest racers gather. Crash, Coco, Cortex, Dingodile — all of them stare at the starting line. Then they stare at you.

You buckle your helmet. You've raced every track, dodged every missile, perfected every drift. This is your moment.

But the question isn't whether you'll race Oxide. It's how.

⚑ Decision Point 1 — Your Approach
Hub World · Strategy

Oxide has set a condition: collect four trophies from across the hub worlds to earn the right to face him. Nothing more is required.

But scattered across every track are relics, CTR tokens, and hidden time trials — challenges that push every racer to the absolute limit.

You weigh your options. The planet's fate hangs in the balance, but so does your legacy.

🏆 Trophy Focus — The Fast Lane
Gem Cup · N. Sanity Beach

You tear through the first hub world, N. Sanity Beach, ignoring the relic ghosts haunting side passages and the token challenges blinking from the cliff faces.

First trophy. Then second. You're in the zone — no distractions, no detours. The other racers watch you blur past with a mix of admiration and concern.

By the time you arrive at the Glacier Park hub, you've collected three trophies and Oxide's arena is blinking on your map. One more cup to go.

But something nags at you. You notice a set of relic pads glowing at the entrance to an ancient track. It would only take a few extra minutes…

🚀 No Looking Back
Glacier Park · Final Trophy

You shake off the temptation. Earth needs saving now. You blast through the final cup race, drifting every corner perfectly, boosting through every shortcut.

Fourth trophy secured. Oxide's challenge portal opens with a blinding flash.

You gun the engine and drive straight into it.

👾 Final Race — Oxide Station
Boss Race · Nitros Oxide

Oxide's circuit is a nightmare — anti-gravity banks, plasma beams, and a track surface slicker than ice. His kart is powered by alien technology you've never seen.

You're good. You're really good. But you haven't practiced the precision turns that deep relic runs would've sharpened.

The race is brutal. Oxide leads for two full laps. On the final stretch, you find one last burst of speed — a desperate power slide that sends you across the finish line just inches ahead.

You win. Barely.

Oxide sputters. His saucer retreats. Earth is safe.

⏱ Quick Relic Run
Side Challenge · Glacier Park

You swing off the main route and hit the relic pad. The ghost of a previous racer shimmers ahead — you have to beat their time across the track while avoiding crates.

Two relics. Three. You find your rhythm and snag a Platinum Relic — something you didn't expect. The side tunnel spits you back out near the trophy race entrance, sharper and faster than before.

🔥 Decision Point 3 — Sharpen Your Edge
Time Trials · Multiple Tracks

You spend the next stretch running time trials on the tracks you rushed through earlier. Each lap teaches you something new — a hidden shortcut here, a perfect drift angle there.

The other racers clock your improvement. Even Coco nods in grudging respect.

You've now beaten three track ghosts and earned two extra relics. Your kart feels dialed in. But Oxide is waiting, and every hour you train is an hour his saucer looms overhead.

⚠ Too Much of a Good Thing
Late Training · Fatigue

Hours blur together. You run the same tracks over and over, chasing ghost times that keep getting shorter by milliseconds.

When you finally enter Oxide's portal, your wrists are stiff and your focus is frayed. The race starts — you're fast, technically perfect — but the spark is gone.

You win on muscle memory alone. Oxide is beaten. Earth is saved. But you don't feel joy — just exhaustion.

👾 Final Race — Oxide Station (Prepared)
Boss Race · Nitros Oxide

The portal opens. Oxide grins from behind his visor — six eyes gleaming with contempt.

The race is everything you prepared for. Hard banks. Plasma walls. Invisible boost pads that only reveal themselves to racers who've studied the track.

You studied. You know exactly where to cut the corners. You hit every boost perfectly. Oxide pushes back on lap three, unleashing a burst of alien speed — but your extra relic training shaved just enough off your lap times.

You cross the line. Champion. It wasn't easy. But it was earned.

🗺 Completionist Route — Every Challenge
Hub World · Deep Dive

You don't just race. You hunt. Relic pads. Token challenges. Hidden warp rooms. Crystal caves. You methodically work through every corner of every hub world.

Days pass. You collect relics from Papu's Pyramid, earn all three tokens on Dingo Canyon, discover the secret shortcut in Cortex Castle.

The other racers are impressed. Some join you for extra laps. Oxide, watching from orbit, seems… unsettled.

You've gathered trophies, relics, and a bag of rare CTR tokens. Now a new door appears in the hub world — a hidden warp room unlocked only by those who truly explored.

🌀 The Secret Warp Room
Hidden Area · Bonus Tracks

The warp room hums with strange energy. Three bonus tracks pulse on the teleporters — tracks that don't appear in any standard cup. These are the developer's trials, the hidden gauntlet left for only the most devoted racers.

You step up to the first pad. A skull icon flashes. These tracks are brutal.

💎 One Last Trial
Bonus Track · One Clear

You choose the hardest-looking track and run it once. It's a twisted, narrow circuit with bouncing TNT crates and walls that breathe. You barely survive. But you finish first.

A gem glints on the finish line — a hidden gem, the rarest collectible. You pocket it. Time to end this.

🏅 Decision Point 4 — The Full Gauntlet
Bonus Tracks · Three Clears

Three tracks. Three victories. You survive lava fields, underwater caverns, and a track that loops through the inside of a crashed spaceship.

After the third finish line, the warp room glows gold. An extra key appears — a reward for completing the full gauntlet. You hold it up. It hums.

Back in the hub, you notice one last door. A door nobody else opened. The key fits.

⚡ The True Arena
Hidden Challenge · Final Test

Inside the door is a single track — unmarked on any map. No ghost. No timer. Just you and a nameless racer in a pitch-black kart.

Three laps. The nameless racer matches every move you make. Drafts every drift. Mirrors every boost.

On the final corner, you find something you didn't know you had — a line nobody taught you. A perfect, improvised apex that shaves a fraction of a second. It's enough.

The black kart stops. The arena goes silent. You exhale.

You are ready for Oxide. You are ready for anything.

⏩ Decision Point 2 — Ready Enough?
Hub World · Pre-Boss

You've collected relics from eight tracks, all three CTR tokens on six circuits, and won every trophy cup. Oxide's portal glows ahead.

But glancing at your stats screen, you notice your best lap times are still a few tenths off the world records. A nagging voice asks: is this enough?

🔁 The Endless Loop
Perfection Spiral

You tell yourself: just one more relic. Just one more token. Just one better lap time.

A week passes. Two weeks. Oxide's portal still glows. The other racers urge you to go.

You can't. There's always something slightly better you could do. Another tenth of a second. Another gem shard. The game never truly ends — and neither, it seems, do you.

👾 Final Race — Oxide Station (Veteran)
Boss Race · Nitros Oxide

Every track you've mastered has led to this moment. Oxide sneers as your kart lines up. He clearly underestimates you.

That is his mistake.

You read every corner before it arrives. Your muscle memory handles the plasma banks while your mind stays calm. When Oxide unleashes his alien boost, you've already prepared your counter — a perfect draft that slingshots you ahead.

Lap three. Final straight. You're three kart lengths ahead. Oxide screams in fury. You cross the line.

Earth is saved. You are its champion.

🌟 Final Race — Oxide Station (Legendary)
Boss Race · Nitros Oxide

The portal opens onto Oxide's station — gleaming chrome, crackling plasma walls, the hum of alien engines.

Oxide takes one look at your kart. His six eyes narrow. He feels it. The quiet certainty radiating from your posture. He has faced champions before. He has never faced this.

You race. Every corner is a brushstroke. Every drift is a sentence. The True Arena taught you the line nobody knows, and you use it twice — once on lap one, once on lap three, where it matters most.

Oxide's alien boost fires. He's still two seconds behind.

You cross the finish line. The station goes silent. Oxide's saucer hangs motionless. Then, slowly, it turns and departs.

No words. No argument. Just acknowledgment.

🏁 The End

The world erupts in celebration. Streamers fall. Crowds cheer. Oxide's saucer retreats into the stars.

You stand on the podium, trophy in hand. But your eyes drift to the hub worlds — the relic pads still glowing, the token challenges still unclaimed. The game isn't finished. You aren't finished.

But the planet is safe, and sometimes that's enough.

Ending A
Hollow Victory
You saved the Earth, but you know the full story wasn't told. The relics and tokens remain, waiting. Maybe someday you'll go back.
🏁 The End

The crowd goes wild. You climbed into that final race a little more prepared than you were an hour ago — and it made all the difference.

Oxide doesn't stay to argue. He's never lost by less. He'll be back, you think. And when he is, you'll be ready.

Ending B
Narrow Champion
You detoured just enough to sharpen your edge — and crossed the line by the skin of your teeth. A victory built from last-minute preparation.
🏁 The End

When the celebration dies down, the other racers tell the story of how you beat Oxide. They talk about your relics. Your tokens. The tracks you ran alone at dawn.

You didn't just win. You mastered the game. Every corner, every shortcut, every secret. Earth was never truly at risk — because you were always going to be ready.

Ending C
True Champion
Completionist and conqueror. You finished what the game was truly asking of you, and Oxide never stood a chance.
🏁 The End

Oxide eventually leaves. Not because you beat him — but because he got bored waiting.

Earth is saved by default. You're still on track four, chasing a ghost timer by eight milliseconds.

The other racers wave goodbye. You don't notice. You're restarting the lap.

Ending D
Endless Challenger
Perfection is a horizon — always visible, never reachable. You chose the journey over the destination, and the journey never ends.
🏁 The End

You win. Of course you win. You know every track by heart at this point. Oxide puts up a decent fight.

But standing on the podium, all you feel is the ache in your hands and a deep desire to never see a racetrack again.

You hang up your helmet for a month. When you come back, the tracks feel new again. Maybe that's the real lesson.

Ending E
Burnout
You over-prepared until the joy drained away. Victory tasted like nothing. Sometimes knowing when to stop is the hardest skill of all.
🏁 The End

They will tell this story for generations. Not just that you beat Oxide — but how. The hidden door. The nameless arena. The line nobody knew.

Crash hangs a banner in the hub world. "Earth's Racer." No name needed. Just the legend.

Oxide's broadcast frequency, still active, transmits one final message across the galaxy: "This planet is not to be touched."

Ending F
Legend of Earth
The rarest ending. You found the hidden path, conquered the secret arena, and achieved something no alien ever expected: a racer who transcended the game itself.
